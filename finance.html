📖 How To Use FinanceOS
A step-by-step guide to getting the most out of your financial dashboard.
Step 1
💵 Add Your Income

Go to Money → Income and add every source of money you receive. Include your salary, side hustle, benefits — anything that hits your bank account. Enter the amount, how often you get paid (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly), and the day you get paid.

💡 If you get paid weekly, add 4 separate entries — one per paycheck — so the app knows exactly when money arrives.
Step 2
💳 Set Up Your Cards

Go to Money → Cards and add a card for each spending category. The idea: one card for housing, one for utilities, one for subscriptions, etc. This keeps your money organized by purpose. Enter the last 4 digits, card type, and current balance.

💡 You don't need real separate cards — you can use one card for multiple categories. Cards here are just labels to organize your budget.
Step 3
📄 Enter Your Bills

Go to Money → Bills and add every recurring expense. Include the bill name, amount, the day of the month it's due, which category it falls under, and which card pays it. Mark large bills as "splittable" if you want to pay them in installments.

⚠️ Be thorough — subscriptions, insurance, utilities, groceries, everything. The more complete your bill list, the better the AI advice.
Step 4
🏦 Add Savings Goals

Go to Money → Savings and set up goals for your emergency fund, vacations, big purchases, etc. Enter how much you have saved now and how much you want to put away each month. The app will project when you'll reach your goal.

Step 5
🎯 Review Allocation

Go to Planning → Allocation to see exactly how much to load onto each card each month. It breaks down your income vs. your bills and tells you if you're short or have money left over. This is your monthly money distribution plan.

Step 6
🤖 Use the AI Advisor

Go to AI → Bill Advisor and enter how much cash you have right now. The AI will rank every bill in priority order, tell you which to pay first, which to split, and which can wait. You can also ask it questions in plain English.

✅ Use the AI Advisor every payday to get a fresh priority plan based on your current cash.
Step 7
✂️ Split Large Bills

Go to Planning → Split Payments to break large bills (like rent or insurance) into smaller payments across multiple paychecks. Choose equal, front-loaded, or back-loaded payment schedules and track each installment.

Step 8
📅 Check Pay Schedule

Go to Planning → Pay Schedule to see a day-by-day calendar of income arriving and bills due. This shows you exactly when money comes in and when it goes out — use it to avoid overdrafts and plan ahead.

🗂️ Category Guide
Every spending category explained — what it covers and what bills belong in it.
🏠
Housing
Priority Tier 1 — Always Pay First

Your shelter costs. Missing these can result in eviction, foreclosure, or late fees with major consequences.

Examples: Mortgage, Rent, HOA fees, Renters insurance, Property taxes, Home repairs
⚡
Utilities
Priority Tier 1 — Always Pay Second

Services that keep your home functioning. Shutoffs cost more to restore than the original bill.

Examples: Electricity (CoServ), Water, Natural Gas, Internet, Cable, Home Phone
🚗
Transport
Priority Tier 2

Getting to work and back. Without transportation you can't earn income.

Examples: Gas, Tolls/NTTA, Car wash, Parking, Bus/train pass, Uber/Lyft budget, Vehicle maintenance
🛡️
Insurance
Priority Tier 2

Protection against financial disasters. A lapse in coverage can cost far more than the premium.

Examples: Car insurance, Life insurance, Health insurance, Dental/Vision, Disability insurance
🏥
Healthcare
Priority Tier 2

Medical costs that can't be delayed without health consequences.

Examples: Prescriptions, Doctor copays, Therapy, Gym/fitness (if medically needed), HSA contributions
💳
Debt Payment
Priority Tier 3

Minimum payments on borrowed money. Missing these damages your credit and adds fees.

Examples: Car note, Credit card minimum payment, Student loan, Buy Now Pay Later (Afterpay), Personal loan
🛒
Groceries
Priority Tier 3

Food and household essentials. Necessary but flexible — you control how much you spend.

Examples: Walmart grocery runs, Sam's Club, Costco, Household supplies, Baby items, Pet food
📱
Subscriptions
Priority Tier 4 — Lowest

Auto-recurring digital services. Easy to forget, easy to cancel. Audit these regularly.

Examples: Cell phone plan, Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, Spotify, YouTube TV, Xbox/PlayStation, Amazon Prime, Adobe, Microsoft 365, Norton, iCloud
🎬
Entertainment
Priority Tier 4

Fun and leisure spending. Important for quality of life but the first to cut when money is tight.

Examples: Dining out, Movies/Cinemark, Concerts, Sports events, Hobbies, Video games, Date nights
📚
Education
Priority Tier 3

Learning and development costs. An investment in your future earning potential.

Examples: Homeschool curriculum (Time4Learning), College tuition, Online courses, Books, Tutoring, School supplies
💄
Personal Care
Priority Tier 4

Self-care and grooming. Necessary but flexible in amount.

Examples: Haircuts, Hair products, Nail salon, Skincare, Barber, Clothing allowance, Gym membership
🏦
Savings
Pay yourself first

Money set aside for future goals. Treat this like a bill — pay it every month without fail.

Examples: Emergency fund, Vacation fund, Down payment, Christmas fund, Personal savings account, Kid's college fund
📦
Other
Miscellaneous

Anything that doesn't fit another category.

Examples: Church tithe/offering, Charitable donations, Gifts, Pet vet bills, HOA fees, Storage unit, Legal fees

💡 The 50/30/20 Rule — A Simple Budget Framework

50%
Needs
Housing, Utilities, Transport, Insurance, Groceries, Debt minimums
30%
Wants
Entertainment, Dining out, Subscriptions, Personal care, Hobbies
20%
Savings & Investing
Emergency fund, Retirement (401k/IRA), Savings goals, Extra debt payoff
